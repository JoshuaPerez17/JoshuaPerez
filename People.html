Panagiotis G Simos

University of Crete

Dr. Panagiotis Simos holds BA (1990) and MA (1993) degrees in Psychology, and a PhD in Experimental Psychology-Biopsychology from Southern Illinois University (1995). He served as Assist. (1996-2001) and Assoc. Professor (2001-2003) at the Dept. of Neurosurgery, Univ. of Texas-Houston Medical School. He is currently Professor of Developmental Neuropsychology, School Of Medicine, UoC and Adjunct Professor at the Department of Pediatrics, University of Tennessee Health Science Center. His research has been funded with US and National grants. He serves as editorial board member and reviewer for several scientific journals and funding organizations. He has published more than 135 research papers in top peer-reviewed journals in Neuropsychology and Neurology, and his work has received more than 3000 citations. He has developed and adapted several psychometric instruments for cognitive and linguistic abilities in children including batteries for executive functions, receptive and expressive language, and general ability.

Fofi Constantinidou

University of Cyprus

In 2006, Dr. Constantinidou joined the Department of Psychology at the University of Cyprus where she directs the Center for Applied Neuroscience, the premier research center for the behavioral sciences in Cyprus.Her research, teaching, and clinical expertise center on acquired adult neurological disorders and their effects on language, learning, memory, executive systems, and categorization. She has published extensively in this area and has received several grants for her work, including funding for clinical trials research from the National Institutes of Health (USA), the Cyprus Research Promotion Foundation, and the European Union.
